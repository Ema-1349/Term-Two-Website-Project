Overview

Roblox is a massively multiplayer online video game that allows users to play a wide variety of different types of games created by other users. The beta version of this game was created by the co-founders David Baszucki and Erik Cassel in 2004, originally under the name BynaBlocks. In 2005. The company officially changed its name to Roblox and launched on September 1, 2006. You are able to design and publish your own games as well, which are coded under an Object-Oriented Programming system utilizing the programming language Lua to manipulate the environment of the game. You are able to create game passes, clothes, and accessories, body parts, gear, packages, and more. These can be sold through Roblox for Robux, the in-game currency. The in-game chat system, monitored by the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), makes it children friendly and blocks out any inappropriate content on the chat. Encouraging friendship and social interactions within the game for children of all ages.

Robux

Robux is the virtual currency in Roblox that allows players to buy various items. Players can obtain Robux by purchasing it with real-life money, from a recurring Robux stipend given to members with the premium membership, and from other players by selling virtual content on Roblox. Clothes can be bought by anyone, but only players with membership can sell them. Only Roblox administrators can see accessories, body parts, gear, and packages un the official Roblox user account. Items with a limited edition status can only be traded between or sold by users with premium membership status. Developers on the site can create various items to be sole through one-time purchases, as well as micro-transactions through developer products. Developers on the site exchange Robux earned from various products on their games into real-world currency through the Developer Exchange system and a percentage of the revenue from purchases is split between the developer and Roblox.
